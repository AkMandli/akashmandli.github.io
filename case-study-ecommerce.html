01 — Executive Summary

A Business Built to Scale —
Held Back by Its Own Systems

A mid-market software and hardware solutions company with $19.2 million in annual revenue and a 200-person workforce was losing competitive ground — not because of its products, but because of the fragmented digital infrastructure holding its commerce operations together. Disconnected systems, manual workflows, an outdated CMS, and the absence of real-time data were creating friction at every stage of the customer journey.

Led engagement transformed this landscape. Through rigorous discovery, cross-functional stakeholder alignment, and meticulous requirements engineering, the project delivered a fully integrated WooCommerce and Salesforce ecosystem on WordPress — and with it, results that redefined the company's digital performance benchmarks.

"Served as the connective tissue between what the business needed and what technology could deliver — ensuring every decision was grounded in data, stakeholder clarity, and commercial outcomes."

02 — Business Challenge

Six Fault Lines in
a Growing Business

The client's digital operations faced compounding challenges that were actively costing revenue and customer trust. Each gap, while identifiable in isolation, was deeply intertwined — creating a systemic problem that demanded strategic, not just technical, intervention.

▲

Fragmented system architecture — Disparate tools and platforms operated in silos, preventing any single source of operational truth across commerce, CRM, and fulfillment.

▲

No unified backend integration — Salesforce and fulfillment systems were unconnected, creating manual handoffs, data duplication, and costly reconciliation overhead.

▲

Zero real-time data sync — Orders, inventory levels, and quotes were subject to significant delays, undermining customer experience and operational agility.

▲

Performance and scalability bottlenecks — The existing Kontent.ai CMS was no longer fit for purpose, delivering slow page loads and a rigid structure ill-suited to commerce growth.

▲

Poor SEO architecture — A lack of structured content organisation and technical SEO hygiene limited organic discoverability at precisely the moment the business needed inbound growth.

▲

Limited growth enablement — With no Cart-to-Quote functionality or B2B-ready commerce flows, the platform was unable to service the full breadth of the client's customer base effectively.

Without a Business Analyst capable of connecting stakeholder pain to system requirements, the transformation risked becoming a technology project in search of a strategy.

03 — Discovery & Analysis

Listening Before Building:
The Discovery Mandate

Initiated a structured discovery programme designed to surface not only what was broken, but why — and what the business truly needed rather than simply what had been assumed. This distinction became foundational to the project's success.

Stakeholder Workshops

Facilitated sessions with commercial, technical, and operations leadership to surface competing priorities and align on shared success criteria.

Technical Debt Audit

Mapped existing system dependencies, integration points, and failure modes to quantify the true cost of inaction and inform remediation sequencing.

Operational Gap Analysis

Traced end-to-end fulfilment and sales workflows to identify process inefficiencies amplified by the absence of data connectivity.

Feasibility Assessment

Conducted both technical and operational feasibility analysis to pressure-test proposed solutions before any commitment was made to build.

This phase established the analytical foundation that guided every subsequent decision — ensuring the solution was scoped to solve the real problem, not just the most visible one.

04 — Requirements & Architecture

Translating Business Intent
into Technical Blueprint

With discovery complete, taken responsibility for translating nuanced stakeholder needs into a precise, actionable specification set. This is where the strategic value of business analysis most clearly materialised — bridging the language of commerce and the logic of engineering.

I did not simply document what was requested. They challenged assumptions, interrogated edge cases, and ensured that every requirement was traceable to a defined business outcome.

A comprehensive documentation suite was prepared to govern the build phase and eliminate ambiguity between teams:

Business Requirements Document (BRD)
Minutes of Meeting (MoMs)
High-Level System Architecture
Data Flow Diagrams
Functional Specifications
Integration Requirements

The high-level architecture design defined how WordPress, WooCommerce, and Salesforce would interact — establishing clear data ownership, event triggers, and synchronisation logic that would underpin the real-time integration. This artefact became the shared reference point for developers, QA engineers, and business stakeholders throughout delivery.

05 — Implementation & Collaboration

Orchestrating Execution
Across Teams and Systems

With architecture approved and requirements baselined, the implementation phase commenced under the ongoing oversight by me — who served as the operational bridge between business stakeholders and the delivery team throughout the build.

01
CMS Migration: Kontent.ai → WordPress & WooCommerce

Content and commerce infrastructure was migrated to a unified WordPress environment, significantly improving flexibility, performance headroom, and third-party compatibility.

02
WooCommerce–Salesforce Real-Time Integration

A structured backend integration layer was implemented to synchronise order data, inventory updates, and customer records — eliminating manual touchpoints and processing delays.

03
Cart-to-Quote Functionality

A bespoke Cart-to-Quote workflow was designed and deployed to support both B2B and B2C purchasing models, enabling sales teams to issue quotes directly from cart interactions without leaving the platform ecosystem.

04
Performance & SEO Architecture

The new CMS platform enabled structured content architecture, metadata management, and page speed improvements — laying the groundwork for sustainable organic discoverability at scale.

05
Ongoing Stakeholder Alignment

Throughout delivery, maintained continuous stakeholder communication, managing change requests, resolving ambiguities in real time, and ensuring acceptance criteria were met at every milestone.

06 — Business Impact

Results That Redefined
the Business Case

The post-implementation outcomes validated the investment in structured business analysis. By ensuring every technical decision was anchored to a commercial requirement, the project delivered results that extended far beyond system modernisation.

150%
Conversion Rate Increase

A seamless, high-performance storefront with B2B-ready quoting removed friction from the buyer journey at every stage.

40%
Faster Order Fulfillment

Real-time WooCommerce–Salesforce sync eliminated manual order processing steps, accelerating dispatch and reducing error rates.

70%
Site Performance Improvement

The WordPress migration delivered measurable gains in page load speed, contributing to lower bounce rates and improved search rankings.

60%
Customer Retention Improvement

Unified data, accurate inventory visibility, and a consistent omnichannel experience strengthened customer trust and repeat purchase behaviour.

Collectively, these outcomes represent a fundamental shift in the company's digital capability — from a platform that constrained growth to one that actively enables it.

07 — Key Takeaways

What This Transformation
Proves

Beyond the metrics, this engagement illustrates durable principles for eCommerce transformation at scale — and for the irreplaceable role that structured business analysis plays within it.

1

Analysis before architecture. Investing in structured discovery prevents costly assumptions from being hardcoded into technical systems. Every week spent in discovery saved weeks of rework in delivery.

2

Integration is a business problem, not just a technical one. Real-time data sync succeeded because its requirements were mapped to operational outcomes — not just API specifications.

3

Documentation is decision-making infrastructure. BRDs, MoMs, and architecture artefacts created alignment across teams and ensured every stakeholder worked from a shared understanding.

4

B2B and B2C can coexist — with the right design. Cart-to-Quote enabled the client to serve enterprise and consumer segments from a single platform, unlocking new revenue pathways.

5

The Business Analyst is a value multiplier. By bridging business intent and technical execution, the BA ensured development effort was never wasted on the wrong problem — maximising ROI across every workstream.
